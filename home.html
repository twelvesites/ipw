Infection Prevention Week

What is Infection Prevention Week?

Infection Prevention Week is an annual event that aims to raise awareness about the importance of infection prevention and control. It is a time to highlight the role of healthcare professionals, patients, and the community in preventing the spread of infections.

Steps to Prevent Infections

  • Hand Hygiene: Wash your hands regularly with soap and water for at least 20 seconds. Use hand sanitizer if soap and water are not available.
  • Use Personal Protective Equipment (PPE): Wear masks, gloves, and other protective gear as needed, especially in healthcare settings.
  • Vaccination: Stay up-to-date with recommended vaccines to protect yourself and others from infectious diseases.
  • Clean and Disinfect: Regularly clean and disinfect frequently touched surfaces and objects.
  • Stay Home When Sick: If you are feeling unwell, stay home to prevent spreading illness to others.
  • Practice Respiratory Hygiene: Cover your mouth and nose with a tissue or your elbow when coughing or sneezing. Dispose of tissues properly.
  • Safe Food Practices: Handle and prepare food safely to avoid foodborne illnesses.
  • Antibiotic Stewardship: Use antibiotics only when prescribed by a healthcare professional and complete the full course of treatment.
